## **🎥 Production Guidelines**

### **Video Format Standards**

#### **Technical Specifications**
- **Resolution**: 1920x1080 (Full HD) minimum, 4K preferred
- **Frame Rate**: 30fps standard, 60fps for smooth motion
- **Audio**: 48kHz/24-bit minimum, stereo output
- **Format**: MP4 with H.264 encoding for compatibility
- **Bitrate**: 8-12 Mbps for 1080p, 25-40 Mbps for 4K

#### **Screen Recording Settings**
- **PostVelocity Platform**: Record at 1920x1080 for clarity
- **Browser**: Use Chrome in full-screen mode
- **Cursor**: Highlight cursor for visibility
- **Zoom**: Use browser zoom (125-150%) for better visibility of small text
- **Clean Interface**: Hide bookmarks, use incognito mode

### **Filming Techniques**

#### **On-Camera Segments**
**Camera Positioning:**
- Eye-level camera height
- Subject positioned slightly off-center (rule of thirds)
- 3-4 feet distance from camera for professional framing
- Consistent background (branded or neutral)

**Presenter Guidelines:**
- Professional attire appropriate for business audience
- Good posture and confident body language
- Natural gestures that don't go outside frame
- Direct eye contact with camera
- Energetic but professional delivery tone

#### **Screen Recording Best Practices**
**Interface Preparation:**
- Clear browser cache and cookies
- Close unnecessary tabs and applications
- Use consistent browser window size
- Hide desktop wallpaper and icons
- Turn off notifications during recording

**Demonstration Flow:**
- Smooth, deliberate mouse movements
- Pause briefly after each click
- Highlight important interface elements
- Use consistent navigation patterns
- Allow time for page loads and processing

### **Script Delivery Techniques**

#### **Natural Presentation Style**
- **Conversational Tone**: Talk to the viewer, not at them
- **Pacing**: Moderate speed with strategic pauses
- **Emphasis**: Stress key points and benefits
- **Energy**: Maintain enthusiasm without being overwhelming
- **Clarity**: Pronounce technical terms clearly

#### **Handling Demonstrations**
- **Setup Context**: Explain what you're about to show
- **Step-by-Step**: Break complex processes into clear steps
- **Real-Time**: Show actual results, not just interface
- **Mistakes**: If errors occur, address them naturally
- **Summaries**: Recap what was accomplished

## **📹 Recording Process**

### **Multi-Camera Setup**

#### **Camera Angles**
**Angle 1 - Primary (Wide)**
- Full upper body shot of presenter
- Professional background
- Main camera for introduction and explanations

**Angle 2 - Close-up**
- Head and shoulders shot
- Use for emphasis and key points
- Backup camera if primary fails

**Angle 3 - Screen Capture**
- PostVelocity platform demonstration
- High-resolution screen recording
- Synchronized with presenter audio

#### **Recording Workflow**
1. **Start all cameras simultaneously**
2. **Record room tone** (30 seconds of silence for audio editing)
3. **Slate each section** (announce "Section 1 - Introduction")
4. **Complete full takes** (avoid stopping mid-section when possible)
5. **Multiple takes** (record backup takes for critical sections)
6. **B-roll capture** (additional screen recordings for editing flexibility)

### **Audio Recording**

#### **Primary Audio Capture**
- **Direct Input**: USB microphone to computer
- **Monitoring**: Use headphones to monitor audio quality
- **Levels**: Keep audio peaks around -12dB to -6dB
- **Room Treatment**: Record in quiet space with minimal echo

#### **Backup Audio**
- **Secondary Recording**: Phone or digital recorder as backup
- **Sync Method**: Clap hands for sync point in post-production
- **Redundancy**: Better to have too much audio than too little

### **Recording Session Management**

#### **Session Organization**
**File Naming Convention:**
- PV_Video01_Overview_Take01_Cam1.mp4
- PV_Video01_Overview_Take01_Audio.wav
- PV_Video01_Overview_Take01_Screen.mp4

**Take Management:**
- Record complete sections rather than entire video in one take
- Mark good takes during recording session
- Note any technical issues or mistakes
- Keep all takes until final edit is complete

#### **Quality Control During Recording**
- **Monitor audio levels** continuously
- **Check video framing** periodically
- **Watch for technical issues** (freezing, lag, etc.)
- **Review key segments** immediately after recording
- **Backup files** to secondary storage immediately

## **✂️ Post-Production Editing**

### **Video Editing Workflow**

#### **Import and Organization**
1. **Create Project Structure**
   - Video files folder
   - Audio files folder
   - Graphics and assets folder
   - Export folder for final videos

2. **Import All Media**
   - All camera angles
   - Screen recordings
   - Audio files
   - Graphics and logos
   - Background music (if applicable)

3. **Sync Footage**
   - Synchronize multi-camera footage
   - Align audio with video
   - Create multicam clips for easier editing

#### **Editing Process**
**Rough Cut (First Pass):**
- Assemble basic structure following script
- Choose best takes for each segment
- Remove obvious mistakes and long pauses
- Establish overall pacing and flow

**Fine Cut (Second Pass):**
- Refine timing and pacing
- Add smooth transitions between segments
- Balance audio levels throughout
- Color correct video footage
- Add screen recording segments

**Final Cut (Third Pass):**
- Add graphics, titles, and lower thirds
- Include PostVelocity branding elements
- Fine-tune audio with noise reduction
- Add background music (if appropriate)
- Create smooth fade-ins and fade-outs

### **Graphics and Visual Elements**

#### **Branding Elements**
**Required Graphics:**
- PostVelocity logo (intro and outro)
- Video title and subtitle
- Section dividers
- Lower thirds for presenter identification
- Call-to-action graphics (subscribe, visit website)

**Optional Graphics:**
- Animated transitions
- Feature highlight overlays
- Progress indicators for longer videos
- Social media handles and contact information

#### **Screen Recording Enhancement**
- **Cursor Highlighting**: Make cursor more visible
- **Zoom Effects**: Highlight specific interface elements
- **Callout Boxes**: Draw attention to important buttons or features
- **Smooth Transitions**: Between different platform sections
- **Loading Overlays**: Cover long processing times with explanatory text

### **Audio Post-Production**

#### **Audio Enhancement**
- **Noise Reduction**: Remove background hiss and room tone
- **EQ**: Enhance voice clarity and warmth
- **Compression**: Even out audio levels
- **De-essing**: Reduce harsh 'S' sounds
- **Normalization**: Consistent volume levels throughout

#### **Audio Elements**
- **Intro Music**: Professional, upbeat (15-30 seconds)
- **Background Music**: Subtle, non-distracting (optional)
- **Sound Effects**: Button clicks, notification sounds for screen demos
- **Outro Music**: Professional wrap-up (15-30 seconds)
